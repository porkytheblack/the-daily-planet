Brand Guidelines v1.0 — dterminal / The Daily Planet

Every edition,
delivered.

The Daily Planet's brand is built on one idea: a newsroom that runs while you sleep. The ink, the broadsheet, the press — these aren't nostalgia. They're the signal that something worth reading has arrived.

01
Editorial authority

Every story is researched, verified, and edited before it reaches you. The brand carries the weight of that process.

02
Ink, not pixels

The design language references print — broadsheets, column rules, mastheads, bylines. Digital, but with the gravity of paper.

03
The press never stops

Editions arrive on schedule. Breaking news interrupts. The newsroom is always working, whether you're watching or not.

04
Your paper, your desk

This is a personal newsroom. The topics, the writers, the voice — all configured by you. The brand serves the reader, not the crowd.

Drop caps

Lead articles use an Instrument Serif drop cap at ~5rem on the first paragraph. This is the only place Instrument Serif appears below headline level.

Instrument Serif

Masthead title, article headlines, section numbers (I, II, III), pull quotes, writer names in bios, topic card names. Italic for emphasis in headlines and editorial accent.

IBM Plex Mono

Bylines, timestamps, category labels, source citations, edition metadata, navigation, dashboard UI, agent status indicators, all data-bearing text.

Category eyebrows, byline/timestamp rows, source cards, masthead meta, dashboard labels, breaking labels

Article body copy, headlines, pull quotes. Extended reading in mono is painful — keep it to labels and data

Space Grotesk

Article body paragraphs, lead decks, annotation body text, sidebar descriptions, dashboard description text. The reading font — everything the user actually reads at length.

Use for

Never for

Headlines, UI labels, code, navigation. Don't use at 600+ weight

03 — Motion

The rhythm of
the press room.

Motion in the Daily Planet serves the reading experience. It should feel like pages turning, not interfaces loading.

Edition Pulse
The newsroom heartbeat
The green dot in the masthead and sidebar. It says: agents are working, sources are being scanned, the next edition is coming. Inherited from Signal's signal pulse.
Breaking Ticker
Continuous horizontal crawl
The breaking news ticker scrolls continuously at a steady pace. Linear easing — it should feel mechanical and relentless, like a wire service feed. Never stops, never speeds up.
EU passes AI act — 90 days to comply   ·   Open models close the gap — benchmarks shift   ·   EU passes AI act — 90 days to comply   ·   Open models close the gap — benchmarks shift   ·  
Story Stagger
Edition items arrive in sequence
When an edition loads, story cards appear in priority order with a 120ms stagger. Each card fades up 10px. The effect says: the editor has ranked these, and they arrive in that order.
Reading Progress
The 2px rust line
On full-page pieces, a 2px rust bar tracks scroll progress across the top of the viewport. It grows with the reader's position — patient, accurate, never jumping ahead.
Card Lift
Story cards rise on hover
In the broadsheet grid, story cards translate -2px on hover. No scale, no bounce. Just a subtle lift that says "this is clickable" without shouting.
Breaking Dot
Urgent status blink
The breaking news label includes a pulsing dot — faster than the edition pulse (1s vs 2s cycle). It creates urgency without alarm. Once the breaking story is no longer new, the dot stops.
Breaking
STANDARD
cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)

Page entry, stagger reveals, card lifts. The default for anything that appears or moves.

DECELERATE
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)

Reading progress bar, loading indicators. Things that approach completion and slow into their final state.

INTERACTIVE
cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)

Hover states, toggle switches, button feedback. Immediate response that feels connected to the input.

LINEAR
linear

Breaking ticker, continuous scrolling elements. Anything that should feel mechanical and constant — the wire feed.

Motion anti-patterns
Never

Spring/bounce physics. Newspapers don't bounce. Nothing bounces.

Never

Parallax scrolling on article content. The reader is here to read, not to be impressed.

Never

Animated data visualizations that play automatically. Charts appear complete. The data is already there.

Never

Page transitions between articles. Each page loads clean — no slide, no fade between routes.

04 — Voice & Tone

Reporting,
not performing.

The Daily Planet speaks like a seasoned editor — authoritative because of accuracy, not volume. The product voice is distinct from the writer voices.

Editorial authority
States what the reporting found.
Doesn't editorialize on its own importance.
DO "5 articles. 18 sources verified. Morning edition."
NO "We've put together an amazing morning edition with 5 incredible stories!"
DO "Breaking: EU passes AI transparency act."
NO "🚨 BREAKING NEWS! You won't believe what the EU just did!"
Source-aware
Every claim has a citation.
Uncertainty is stated, not hidden.
DO "9 sources verified by Lead Editor."
NO "We researched this thoroughly."
DO "Analysts note the market is pricing in a pause — the direction remains contested."
NO "The market will definitely pivot to cuts in Q3."
Agent transparency
The newsroom agents are visible.
Their work is shown, not hidden.
DO "Lead Researcher scanning 47 sources. Jules Nakamura drafting afternoon update."
NO "Your content is being prepared." (vague, hides the process)
DO "Written by Mara Kessler · Senior Correspondent · Style: Analytical, Evidence-First"
NO "Generated by AI" (accurate but reductive — the writer has a name and a voice)
Writer voices are separate
The product voice is the editor's.
Each writer has their own.
DO Product UI: utilitarian, precise, short. "Filed 6:42 AM · Verified by Editor."
DO Writer prose: whatever the user defined. Analytical, narrative, concise, textured — each writer is different.
NO Mixing product voice into articles. The editor's annotations should be distinct from the writer's prose.
NO All writers sounding the same. If they do, the voice descriptions need more differentiation.

Stroke rules

Always 1.5px stroke-width. Round linecap, round linejoin. No fill except the edition-green dot for active status. Viewbox: 28×28.

Color rules

Rust (#8B5A2B) on light backgrounds. Concrete (#F4F1EC) on dark. Edition Green is reserved for live status only — never for icons.

Never

Filled icons. Rounded-corner containers. Emoji in any editorial context. Icons from packs without adapting to 1.5px stroke weight.

Editorial grid

Max content width: 1100px (broadsheet/editorial) or 900px (dispatch/full-page prose). Page padding: 3rem desktop / 1.25rem mobile. The gap-as-border pattern from Signal carries into all grids — story cards, data rows, source blocks, dashboard panels.

Article prose column max-width: 620px. This ensures comfortable line length (55–70 characters) at the body text size. Annotation sidebar: 260px. Section marker column: 180px.

Spacing principles
DO Generous whitespace around article prose. The reader needs room to think.
DO Dense metadata in mastheads and sidebars — this is utilitarian space, not reading space.
NO Equal spacing everywhere. The broadsheet is deliberately asymmetric — main column wider than sidebar.
NO Line-height under 1.7 for article body text. This isn't a dashboard — people are reading paragraphs here.
